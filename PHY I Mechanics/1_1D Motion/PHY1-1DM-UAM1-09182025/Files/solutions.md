## **Bicyclist starting from rest — 1**

1. **Physics & task**
   Straight-line motion with **constant acceleration** ⇒ kinematic equations apply. From the story: starts from rest, reaches **6.0 m/s** in **4.0 s**. **Find the acceleration** (magnitude along the motion).

2. **Kinematic table**

| Quantity      | Symbol     |             Value | Given?                        |
| ------------- | ---------- | ----------------: | ----------------------------- |
| Displacement  | $\Delta x$ |                 — | not needed                    |
| Initial speed | $v_i$      | $0.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Implicit (“starts from rest”) |
| Final speed   | $v_f$      | $6.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Explicit                      |
| Acceleration  | $a$        |               $?$ | Solve for                     |
| Time interval | $t$        |   $4.0\,\text{s}$ | Explicit                      |

3. **Equation choice**
   Use $v_f = v_i + a t$.

4. **Algebra**
   $a = \dfrac{v_f - v_i}{t}$.

5. **Numbers & units**
   $a = \dfrac{6.0 - 0.0}{4.0}\,\text{m/s}^2 = 1.5\,\text{m/s}^2$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{1.5\ \text{m/s}^2}$

## **Bicyclist starting from rest — 2**

1. **Physics & task**
   Constant-acceleration motion. The rider starts from rest, covers **18 m**, and is moving **6.0 m/s** at that point. **Find the time** to reach that speed.

2. **Kinematic table**

| Quantity      | Symbol     |             Value | Given?     |
| ------------- | ---------- | ----------------: | ---------- |
| Displacement  | $\Delta x$ |  $18.0\,\text{m}$ | Explicit   |
| Initial speed | $v_i$      | $0.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Implicit   |
| Final speed   | $v_f$      | $6.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Explicit   |
| Acceleration  | $a$        |                 — | not needed |
| Time interval | $t$        |               $?$ | Solve for  |

3. **Equation choice**
   Average-speed form (valid for constant $a$): $\Delta x = \dfrac{v_i + v_f}{2}\,t$.

4. **Algebra**
   $t = \dfrac{2\,\Delta x}{v_i + v_f}$.

5. **Numbers & units**
   $t = \dfrac{2(18.0)}{0.0 + 6.0}\,\text{s} = \dfrac{36.0}{6.0}\,\text{s} = 6.0\,\text{s}$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{6.0\ \text{s}}$

## **Car braking to a stop — 1**

1. **Physics & task**
   Constant-acceleration (uniform slowdown). The car goes **27.0 m/s**, stops in **4.5 s**. **Find the (magnitude of) steady slowdown** (acceleration opposite motion).

2. **Kinematic table**

| Quantity      | Symbol     |              Value | Given?           |
| ------------- | ---------- | -----------------: | ---------------- |
| Displacement  | $\Delta x$ |                  — | not needed       |
| Initial speed | $v_i$      | $27.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Explicit         |
| Final speed   | $v_f$      |  $0.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Implicit (stops) |
| Acceleration  | $a$        |                $?$ | Solve for        |
| Time interval | $t$        |    $4.5\,\text{s}$ | Explicit         |

3. **Equation choice**
   $v_f = v_i + a t$.

4. **Algebra**
   $a = \dfrac{v_f - v_i}{t}$.

5. **Numbers & units**
   $a = \dfrac{0.0 - 27.0}{4.5}\,\text{m/s}^2 = -6.0\,\text{m/s}^2$.
   Magnitude of slowdown: $\boxed{6.0\ \text{m/s}^2}$.

## **Car braking to a stop — 2**

1. **Physics & task**
   Constant-acceleration braking. The car at **27.0 m/s** comes to rest over **91.13 m**. **Find the braking time**.

## **Skier descending a slope — 1**

1. **Physics & task**
   Constant acceleration along the slope. The skier starts with **2.0 m/s**, travels **22.5 m** in **5.0 s**. **Find the speed at the end** (magnitude along the slope).

2. **Kinematic table**

| Quantity      | Symbol     |             Value | Given?     |
| ------------- | ---------- | ----------------: | ---------- |
| Displacement  | $\Delta x$ |  $22.5\,\text{m}$ | Explicit   |
| Initial speed | $v_i$      | $2.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Explicit   |
| Final speed   | $v_f$      |               $?$ | Solve for  |
| Acceleration  | $a$        |                 — | not needed |
| Time interval | $t$        |   $5.0\,\text{s}$ | Explicit   |

3. **Equation choice**
   $\Delta x = \dfrac{v_i + v_f}{2}\,t$.

4. **Algebra**
   $v_f = \dfrac{2\,\Delta x}{t} - v_i$.

5. **Numbers & units**
   $v_f = \dfrac{2(22.5)}{5.0} - 2.0 = 9.0 - 2.0 = 7.0\,\text{m/s}$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{7.0\ \text{m/s}}$

## **Ball thrown vertically upward —  1**

1. **Physics & task**
   Vertical straight-line motion under constant gravity. Tossed upward at **6.0 m/s**, slows until it stops at the peak (so $v_f=0$). We want the **time to the top**.

2. **Kinematic table**

| Quantity      | Symbol     |                Value | Given?             |
| ------------- | ---------- | -------------------: | ------------------ |
| Displacement  | $\Delta x$ |                    — | not needed         |
| Initial speed | $v_i$      |    $6.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Explicit           |
| Final speed   | $v_f$      |    $0.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Implicit at peak   |
| Acceleration  | $a$        | $-9.8\,\text{m/s}^2$ | Explicit (gravity) |
| Time interval | $t$        |                  $?$ | Solve for          |

3. **Equation**
   $v_f = v_i + a t$.

4. **Algebra**
   $t = \dfrac{v_f - v_i}{a}$.

5. **Substitute & compute**
   $t = \dfrac{0.0 - 6.0}{-9.8}\,\text{s} = 0.61\,\text{s}$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{0.61\ \text{s}}$

## **Ball thrown vertically upward — 2**

1. **Physics & task**
   Vertical straight-line motion under constant gravity. Tossed upward at **8.0 m/s**, comes to rest at the top ($v_f=0$). We want the **maximum height reached**.

2. **Kinematic table**

| Quantity      | Symbol     |                Value | Given?     |
| ------------- | ---------- | -------------------: | ---------- |
| Displacement  | $\Delta x$ |                  $?$ | Solve for  |
| Initial speed | $v_i$      |    $8.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Explicit   |
| Final speed   | $v_f$      |    $0.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Implicit   |
| Acceleration  | $a$        | $-9.8\,\text{m/s}^2$ | Explicit   |
| Time interval | $t$        |                    — | not needed |

3. **Equation**
   $v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2 a \Delta x$.

4. **Algebra**
   $\Delta x = \dfrac{v_f^2 - v_i^2}{2a}$.

5. **Substitute & compute**
   $\Delta x = \dfrac{0.0^2 - (8.0)^2}{2(-9.8)} = \dfrac{-64}{-19.6} \approx 3.27\,\text{m}$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{3.27\ \text{m}}$

## **Train leaving a station — 1**

1. **Physics & task**
   Constant-acceleration start. The train begins from rest, travels **200 m** in **28.28 s**. **Find the steady acceleration** (magnitude along the track).

2. **Kinematic table**

| Quantity      | Symbol     |             Value | Given?     |
| ------------- | ---------- | ----------------: | ---------- |
| Displacement  | $\Delta x$ |   $200\,\text{m}$ | Explicit   |
| Initial speed | $v_i$      | $0.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Implicit   |
| Final speed   | $v_f$      |                 — | not needed |
| Acceleration  | $a$        |               $?$ | Solve for  |
| Time interval | $t$        | $28.28\,\text{s}$ | Explicit   |

3. **Equation choice**
   $\Delta x = v_i t + \tfrac12 a t^2$ → with $v_i=0$: $\Delta x = \tfrac12 a t^2$.

4. **Algebra**
   $a = \dfrac{2\,\Delta x}{t^2}$.

5. **Numbers & units**
   $a = \dfrac{2(200)}{(28.28)^2}\,\text{m/s}^2 \approx \dfrac{400}{799.8}\,\text{m/s}^2 \approx 0.50\,\text{m/s}^2$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{0.50\ \text{m/s}^2}$

## **Train leaving a station — 2**

1. **Physics & task**
   Constant-acceleration start. The train begins from rest, covers **300 m** with a steady pickup of **$0.60\,\text{m/s}^2$**. **Find the speed at the end** of that stretch (magnitude).

2. **Kinematic table**

| Quantity      | Symbol     |                Value | Given?     |
| ------------- | ---------- | -------------------: | ---------- |
| Displacement  | $\Delta x$ |      $300\,\text{m}$ | Explicit   |
| Initial speed | $v_i$      |    $0.0\,\text{m/s}$ | Implicit   |
| Final speed   | $v_f$      |                  $?$ | Solve for  |
| Acceleration  | $a$        | $0.60\,\text{m/s}^2$ | Explicit   |
| Time interval | $t$        |                    — | not needed |

3. **Equation choice**
   $v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2 a \Delta x$.

4. **Algebra**
   $v_f = \sqrt{v_i^2 + 2 a \Delta x}$.

5. **Numbers & units**
   $v_f = \sqrt{0.0^2 + 2(0.60)(300)}\,\text{m/s} = \sqrt{360}\,\text{m/s} \approx 18.97\,\text{m/s}$.

**Answer:** $\boxed{18.97\ \text{m/s}}$
